⚡ Athlete OS · Pilot Playbook

Run your first real cohort — and know if it worked.

Not a beta test. A hand-picked squad of athletes, logging honestly, with a coach who acts on what he sees. Here's exactly how to run it this week.

The one-line goal

Get 5–6 hand-picked athletes checking in honestly 4+ days a week for four straight weeks — and walk away with at least one athlete who can name a real thing he did differently because the app was watching. That's the whole win. Not signups. Not perfect streaks. Honest logging that survives a bad week, and a coach who acts on it.

For Aiman · UNC — Sydney
01

Who to get on board

You're not recruiting talent. You're recruiting phone-openers — people who'll log honestly every day. A gifted athlete who won't check in gives you zero. A mid-tier grinder who logs daily gives you everything.

Your ideal first athlete is your MMA/BJJ mate. Already lined up, emotionally invested, and doing the exact sport Athlete OS serves best — soreness, RPE, training load, the "you vs you" creed. His emotionality is a feature: he'll actually use the note field. He becomes your reference case — his streak, his load card, his AI-insight moment are the proof you show everyone else.

Anchor ×1
Your emotional MMA/BJJ mate. Onboard first, in person. He's your runbook and your first story.
Core ×4
Your own basketball players. The ones who do extra reps unprompted and ask "what should I work on?" You already see their on-court performance — that's what makes "the gap shows up on the court" real and enforceable. This group proves retention.
Wildcard ×1
One boxing / BJJ gym contact. Stress-tests that the sport packs reshape drills and language per athlete — your white-label proof later. Max two off-sport athletes; every one you can't watch train weakens the loop.

Cohort size: 6, hard ceiling of 8. Fewer than 4 and you can't spot patterns or build squad energy. More than 8 and your "Needs attention today" list becomes a chore you stop checking — and the pilot dies. Source them face-to-face or by personal voice note. Never a group blast. The personal ask is what converts.

The exact ask — voice note or in person

"I'm building something called Athlete OS — accountability app, it's you against you, tracks how you're actually training. I'm only letting a handful of people in first, and I want you to be a founding athlete. You in?"

Founding, chosen, hand-picked — never "want to test my beta?" That framing is the difference between daily effort and guinea-pig energy.

02

The 4-week plan

Onboard in a staggered wave, not all at once. Anchor first → watch 3–4 days, fix the first friction → then the core four → then the wildcard. Each new athlete joins an app that already has a live streak and squad activity, not an empty room.

Week 0 · The day-0 handover ritual

15 minutes, phone in HIS hand. Don't text the link and hope — sit next to him. Installing for him is the single highest-leverage thing you do all week. iPhone Add-to-Home-Screen and the notification prompt are where solo installs die. Not in the room = didn't happen.

  • Invite link open in his phone browser
  • Share → Add to Home Screen — icon visible on his home screen
  • Notifications Allowed — tested, buzz confirmed
  • First check-in done together — let the celebration fire (flash, XP fly-up, haptic). Say nothing. Let the dopamine land.
  • Coach message sent within 2 hrs — so his next notification is YOU, not the system
  • Day-5 date written down — so you know exactly when to judge activation

Coach the first check-in to be honest and slightly ugly. If he's wrecked — soreness high, energy low. It proves the celebration rewards showing up, not good numbers. Say it to his face: "Inflate this and the gap shows up on the mat — and I see your performance too."

Weeks 1–4 · The fixed rhythm — put both touchpoints in your own calendar
Every Monday
The reset

One message naming the week's focus, tied to his sport.

Every Friday
Load review

Open his weekly Training Load card. Send ONE observation that proves you read it — a spike, a drop-off, a smart back-off he made.

Daily · 2 min
Triage scan

Open the dashboard, scan "Needs attention today," hand-reply to any check-in with a note, send one human message per flag.

After 3rd check-in
Teach the loop

Read his AI insight aloud and message him about it: honest log → AI spots something → coach acts → he feels seen.

Day 7
Sit-down debrief

Show his streak, load card, level. Celebrate the streak explicitly. Set ONE week-2 target together.

Weeks 4–6
The boring middle

Where retention dies. Hold the Monday/Friday rhythm no matter what, and attach a real coach moment to his next level-up so momentum has a horizon.

The day-2/3 cliff: novelty fades 48–72 hrs after signup. No check-in by midday day 2 → same-day nudge. A 3-day streak that breaks is worse than never starting. Write down every friction point from the anchor before you invite anyone else — one athlete onboarded perfectly teaches you the runbook you'll sell.

03

How to keep them logging

Retention isn't the XP loop. It's the athlete feeling seen within hours of a check-in. Everything here protects one thing: honesty. The moment a bad-day log feels like a failure, honest logging dies — and so does the pilot.

→
Reply to the NOTE, never the numbers. The note is the emotional payload. Quote his words back so he knows a person read it. Two minutes a day — your single biggest lever.
→
Celebrate the ugly log LOUDER than the clean one. Energy 2, soreness 8, a rough note — that's the moment for a rating bump or a message. React only to good numbers and you train him to inflate.
→
Never weaponize the streak. "Don't break your streak" turns an identity tool into a guilt tool. A missed day is rest, not failure. The real churn signal is a streak that never restarts — if he's dark 48 hrs, reach out about the human.
→
One notification, not five. A single check-in reminder at a fixed time. Everything else that pings should be a real coach action. If it isn't you being human, question whether it should fire.
→
The creed is HIS line, not your slogan. "The app isn't me watching you — it's you watching you. I see your performance on the mat anyway. The honesty in there is for you."
Steal these — exact coach scripts
Monday reset

"New week. It's you against you — nobody's scoring but you. This week I want one thing: honest RPE after every session, even the flat ones. I'm watching your load, not your ego."

Bad-day reaction · fire on any low-energy / high-soreness log

"This is the log I respect most — you told the truth on a hard day. That's the whole game. Rest up, we go again."

Streak-break reframe · same day he misses

"Streak's a tool, not a jail. A missed day is rest, not failure — I'd take one honest gap over ten fake logs. Check in today and we keep rolling."

Load-spike · duty of care

"App showed me your load climbing and soreness up two days straight — that's how tweaks happen. Thursday's technical, not a war. You're protecting the work, not dodging it."

04

What to measure

Tiny cohort — so treat every number as a per-athlete count, not a percentage. Your entire analytics stack is the "Needs attention today" triage plus three scheduled conversations. No spreadsheets.

Day-7 retention
6 / 6

Day-30 retention
4–5 / 6
Still logging 4+ days/week at day 30.
Cadence
4+ / wk
Honest check-ins per athlete. 5–7 locked in · 3–4 holding · 0–2 intervene. Don't chase a perfect 7 — it invites inflation.
Streak recovery
48 hrs
Every broken streak restarted within two days at least once.
Lagging proof
≥ 1
One athlete names a concrete behaviour that changed — and says he'd be annoyed if you switched it off.
Weekly honesty spot-check
Pick one athlete. Compare logged RPE/soreness against what you saw in the gym. Match = trustworthy data. "Fresh, RPE 4" after a war of a round = the exact coaching conversation the whole product exists to create. Flat-lined inputs (10/0/10 every day) = someone's gaming it — re-sell the honesty tutorial.
The three conversations — book all three on day one or they won't happen
W1
Week 1 · 15 min, per athlete. "Did the check-in take under a minute? Did the celebration land or feel cringe? Straight up — honest numbers or shading them up? What made you skip a day?" Write answers verbatim.
W4
Week 4 · 10 min. "Still worth opening? What do you ignore? Has it changed anything you actually do in training?"
END
End-of-pilot debrief · 20 min. "Do you feel more accountable? Name one thing you did differently because it was watching. Would you be annoyed if I turned it off tomorrow? Would you tell a training partner?" The last two are your go/no-go for expanding.

The dashboard tells you if they're using it. Only the conversations tell you why they'd quit and whether they feel more accountable. Retention without a debrief is a vanity number.

05

After it works

Capture wins in the moment, not as a favour you beg for later. Ask at emotional peaks — right after a level-up, a 7-day streak, a coach message that landed. A 20-second voice note, not a survey. Save every reply into Drive 03 Docs & Guides. Target: 3 usable before/after stories.

Testimonial prompt · after a level-up or 7-day streak

"Bro real quick — what's different about how you're training since you started logging honest every day? Talk into your phone for 20 seconds, no script. Wanna use it if it's fire."

Athlete → teammate referral · hand them a code

"Founding spots are limited and they don't come back. You're in — full badge vault. Send this to one training partner you actually rock with. They get founding too. After the seats fill, everyone else only gets the taster."

The ladder — don't skip a rung
  • Now — your MMA anchor. Instrument + capture his story on day 1 / 7 / 14.
  • The cohort — your 6. Success = 4–5 still checking in weekly at week 4 + 3 before/after stories.
  • A second squad in a different sport (netball, soccer — even 5 athletes) through the same runbook. Two sports proving out = a credible white-label pitch. One sport = "nice app for your gym."
  • Coach #2 — the graduation test. Mint one COACH code, give it to a coach you trust, watch them run their own squad without you touching their dashboard. If they need you in the room, it's not ready to sell.
  • Only then — the white-label build. New Supabase project per client, coach personas per vertical, billing.
Hold the line

No white-label productisation until all three gates are green: (1) cohort hits its week-4 retention number, (2) 3 before/after stories saved, (3) one outside coach ran a squad solo. The "let's invest money into it" pressure is real — resist it until the loop is proven. Scale a leaky bucket and every new coach amplifies churn instead of the win.

Recruit-a-coach pitch line

"Your athletes tell you they trained hard. This tells you the truth — sleep, soreness, effort, honesty — and it flags who's about to break down before they do. It's them against them. You just finally get to see who actually showed up. Want a code to run your own squad?"

06

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Recruiting for talent over daily-logging discipline. A flaky superstar gives you no data and a dead-looking app.
  • Texting the link and letting them self-install. iPhone Add-to-Home-Screen and the push prompt are where solo installs die.
  • Skipping notification permission "to not be pushy." Without push the app is invisible after day 1. Turn it on together, day 0, non-negotiable.
  • Rewarding good numbers with your attention. It silently trains inflation and destroys the one signal the product protects.
  • Weaponizing the streak. An emotional athlete ghosts rather than logs a low day.
  • Over-notifying. More than one automated daily nudge reads as spam and gets push killed.
  • Going quiet in the boring middle (weeks 4–6). Retention dies here without the rhythm and a milestone to climb toward.
  • Reading a 1-athlete cohort as signal. The MMA mate's bad week reads as "pilot failed" when it's just a mood. You need the squad for signal.
  • Skipping the debrief because the dashboard "looks fine." No named behaviour change = no proof of value.
  • Scaling on excitement, not evidence. "20 seats filled" is a success costume. Weekly-active-at-week-4 is the only number that means anything.
